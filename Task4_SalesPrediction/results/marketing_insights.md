# Marketing Insights Report

## Dataset
Source: [Kaggle - Advertising CSV](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/bumba5341/advertisingcsv)

- **Total observations:** 200
- **Average Sales:** 14.0k units
- **Total advertising budget (avg):** $200.9k

## Channel Performance Summary

| Channel | Correlation | Avg Spend | Sales Lift | ROI Rank |
|---------|------------|-----------|------------|----------|
| TV | 0.782 | $147.0k | +7.0k | #2 |
| Radio | 0.577 | $23.3k | +5.2k | #1 |
| Newspaper | 0.229 | $30.6k | +2.3k | #3 |

## Key Findings

### 1. Radio delivers the highest ROI
- Every $1,000 invested in Radio advertising generates approximately **189 additional units** in sales.
- Radio should be the primary advertising channel.

### 2. TV has the strongest correlation with sales
- Correlation coefficient: **0.782**
- High spend on TV is strongly associated with higher sales.

### 3. Newspaper advertising has diminishing returns
- Newspaper has the weakest correlation with sales (0.229).
- Budget allocated to Newspaper may yield better results if redirected to TV or Radio.

## Recommendations

1. **Maximize TV budget** - It provides the highest correlation with sales
2. **Invest in Radio** - Strong ROI, especially for supplementary reach
3. **Minimize Newspaper spend** - Weakest performer; consider digital alternatives
4. **Consider TV + Radio synergy** - Combined campaigns may yield compounding effects
5. **Test budget reallocation** - Shift 30-50% of Newspaper budget to Radio
